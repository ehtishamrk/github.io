The Hidden Cost of Informal HR in Pakistan

The absence of formal HR infrastructure in Pakistani businesses is one of the most expensive invisible costs in the market. It doesn't show up on a balance sheet. But it shows up in turnover rates, in legal disputes, in low productivity, in declining morale, and in the inability to attract quality talent at scale.

When employees don't know their rights and responsibilities, conflict is constant. When performance expectations aren't documented, accountability is impossible. When there's no disciplinary process, managers either do nothing about poor performance or act in legally exposed ways. When HR policies exist only in the founder's head, inconsistency is guaranteed — and inconsistency destroys organizational trust.

Our HR Development and Management service builds the infrastructure that prevents all of this — grounded in Pakistani labor law, calibrated to your specific business, and designed to actually be used, not archived.

Compliance: The Legal Dimension You Cannot Ignore

Pakistan's labor laws impose specific obligations on employers that most SME owners are not fully aware of — and non-compliance creates real legal and financial exposure. Our HR service includes a full compliance review covering:

  • Employees' Old-Age Benefits Institution (EOBI) registration and contribution requirements
  • Provincial Social Security (PESSI/SESSI/KPESI/BESSI) obligations
  • Minimum wage compliance — provincial minimums vary and change regularly
  • Working hours, overtime, and leave entitlements under the Punjab Shops & Establishments Ordinance
  • Employment contract requirements and probation period rules
  • Termination and redundancy procedures under Pakistani law
